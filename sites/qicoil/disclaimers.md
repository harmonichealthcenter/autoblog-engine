# Approved disclaimer language for Qicoil.com

The system prompt and the compliance check both reference these. Edit here if legal review changes the wording.

## Primary disclaimer (mandatory body inclusion when an article makes Qi Coil benefit claims)

> Qi Coil products are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease and have not been evaluated by the FDA.

## Short variant (acceptable in shorter pieces)

> Qi Coil is a wellness device, not a medical device. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

## Consult-physician note (use alongside primary disclaimer in any article touching specific symptoms)

> The information here is for general wellness context, not medical advice. Talk to a qualified healthcare provider about any symptom or condition.

## Where to insert
- Stream A (reputation) articles: required, in body, in a section about what Qi Coil legally is.
- Stream B (biohacking) articles: required only if the article makes a Qi Coil benefit claim. If Qi Coil is mentioned only in passing as one tool among many, the biohacking content does not need the disclaimer.
- Stream C (educational) articles: not required unless the article makes Qi Coil benefit claims (most won't).

## Voice rules
- Insert naturally in body, not as a footer. The disclaimer reads better when it's part of a section explicitly about what Qi Coil is and isn't.
- Do not soften the language. The exact phrasing is the legally relevant phrasing.
- If a sentence elsewhere in the article would otherwise be a health claim, restructure it before adding the disclaimer. The disclaimer does not legitimize a claim that shouldn't be made.
