# How Much Does Qiari Cost in 2026?

Qiari offers three subscription tiers in 2026: Basic at $9.99/month, Core at $19.99/month, and Ultra at $39.99/month, plus a dedicated Practitioner tier for wellness professionals. New users can start with a free trial to test sessions before committing, and annual plans offer modest discounts over month-to-month billing. Most subscribers settle into Basic or Core.

That's the short answer. The longer answer involves what each tier actually unlocks, how Qiari's pricing compares to buying hardware like a Qi Coil, and whether the subscription model genuinely saves money or just spreads cost over time. There's also a strategic logic behind why Qiari is priced the way it is, and it's worth understanding before you pick a tier.

## What's Included in Each Qiari Subscription Tier

Qiari's tiers are structured around session library access, personalization depth, and how often you can run sessions. The jump from one tier to the next isn't about gatekeeping basic functionality, it's about how seriously you're using the platform.

### Basic Plan: The Entry Point

The Basic plan at $9.99/month is designed for people who want to test what device-free frequency wellness actually feels like before going deeper. You get access to a curated set of foundational sessions covering the most common intentions: sleep, calm, focus, and energy. Session length is shorter on average, and personalization is lighter, meaning the app uses your stated intentions to recommend sessions but doesn't go deep on pattern-tracking across weeks.

For most newcomers, Basic is enough to figure out whether the experience resonates. If you're skeptical but curious, this is where to start. The [Qiari signup and first-week guide](https://www.qiari.ai/blog/qiari-signup-first-week-guide) walks through what to expect in those early sessions.

### Core Plan: The Most Popular Choice

Core at $19.99/month is where most active users land. The library expands significantly, with sessions across a broader range of intentions (creativity, emotional release, deep rest, post-workout recovery, focus-block work sessions, and more). Personalization deepens too, with the app starting to adapt recommendations based on which sessions you return to, what time of day you tend to run them, and which intentions cluster together for you.

Core is the tier built for someone who's stopped asking "does this work for me" and started asking "what else can I use this for." If you're running sessions three or more times a week, Core typically pays for itself in variety alone. The [full pricing plan comparison](https://www.qiari.ai/blog/qiari-pricing-plans-comparison) breaks down feature-by-feature differences.

### Ultra Plan: For Serious Practitioners

Ultra at $39.99/month opens the full session library, including longer-form sessions, advanced personalization, and priority access to new session releases. This tier suits people who've integrated frequency sessions into a daily wellness practice and want the deepest customization the platform offers.

Ultra also tends to attract people who already own a Qi Coil and use Qiari as the remote-access complement when traveling or away from their device. If you're curious about that combination, the [Qiari vs Qi Coil comparison](https://www.qiari.ai/blog/qiari-vs-qi-coil-app-or-device) goes into when each one makes sense.

### Practitioner Tier: For Wellness Professionals

The Practitioner tier is priced separately and built for coaches, energy workers, breathwork facilitators, and wellness professionals who want to incorporate Qiari sessions into their client work. It includes multi-user features, session scheduling for clients, and a different licensing structure. If you're a working practitioner, this isn't an upsell of Ultra, it's a different product category.

Others charge $30 to $60/month for highly produced single-modality experiences (sleep-only, meditation-only).

Qiari sits in the middle on price and toward the top on breadth of session types and personalization. ### Why Qiari Costs Less Than Hardware Like Qi Coil

A Qi Coil device runs into the hundreds, sometimes thousands of dollars depending on the model. Qiari at $9.99 to $39.99/month is, by design, a fraction of that. The reason is structural: Qiari is software, hardware costs are real, and David Wong built Qiari specifically to be the low-friction front door for people who aren't ready to commit to a device.

That doesn't mean Qiari is a watered-down Qi Coil. It's a different experience entirely. The app delivers structured session experiences you engage with from anywhere, no hardware required. ### Subscription vs. One-Time Purchase: What Actually Saves Money?

If you ran a Core subscription ($19.99/month) for two full years, you'd spend roughly $480. That's well under the cost of most frequency hardware, and you'd have spent zero on a device that might sit unused if the practice didn't stick. On the other hand, if you know frequency wellness is going to be a long-term daily practice and you want hardware-based sessions, the math eventually tips toward owning a device.

The honest answer: subscription wins for exploration and flexibility, ownership wins for long-term committed use. Most people don't know which camp they're in until they've tried the modality for a few weeks, which is precisely why Qiari exists as the entry point.

## Are There Hidden Fees or Cancellation Costs?

No. Qiari subscriptions are month-to-month or annual, and you can cancel from your account settings without a phone call, retention email gauntlet, or cancellation fee. If you cancel mid-cycle, you retain access until the end of the billing period you've already paid for.

There are no per-session charges, no premium session unlocks inside your tier, and no surprise upgrade prompts mid-session. What you pay for the tier is what you get for the tier.

## Annual Plans: Do They Save You Money?

Annual plans offer a modest discount over paying monthly, typically in the range of one to two months free across a twelve-month commitment. The math is straightforward: if you've already used the free trial and a month or two of monthly billing, and you know the practice is sticking, switching to annual is a small but real saving.

If you're not sure yet, stay monthly. The annual discount isn't large enough to justify locking in before you know the practice is something you'll actually use.

## How to Choose the Right Qiari Plan for Your Budget

The simplest framework: start where the experience is risk-free, and upgrade only when the current tier starts feeling limiting. Most users follow a path that looks like free trial, then Basic for a month or two, then Core when they realize they want more session variety. Very few people start at Ultra unless they already have a daily wellness practice they're integrating Qiari into.

If budget is tight, Basic genuinely delivers the core experience. If budget is flexible and you want the full library from day one, Core is the most common landing point. Ultra is for the committed practitioner.

### Questions to Ask Yourself Before Upgrading

Three questions cut through most of the noise. First: am I running sessions at least three times a week? If not, you probably don't need to upgrade yet. Second: am I hitting the edges of my current tier (running the same sessions repeatedly because I've exhausted what's available)? If yes, the next tier will refresh your practice. Third: have I started using sessions for new intentions I didn't anticipate when I signed up? That usually means deeper personalization (Core or Ultra) will help.
