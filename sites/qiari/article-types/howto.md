# How-To / Explainer Article Type

How-to and explainer articles target informational queries. They answer "how does X work" or "how do I do Y" comprehensively.

## Structure expectations
- 1,200 to 1,600 words.
- 5-7 H2 sections, often in a logical sequence (step 1, step 2, etc. — but worded as substantive subheadings, not "Step 1: Setup").
- Open with the specific moment the reader has the question. Not a generic intro.
- Front-load the answer. Give a concise version in the first 150 words, then expand with detail and nuance.
- Use specific examples wherever possible. "When you start a session, you'll see..." beats "users typically see...".
- Address the "but what if..." questions readers actually have. Skim the H2s of the top 5 ranking articles for that query (mentally) and make sure your article covers what they cover plus at least one thing they don't.
- Closing CTA uses "RESONANCE" once.

## How-to-specific guidance
- The how-to format is heavily commoditized in AI content. Your differentiator is concrete specificity. If a competitor's article says "set your intention", your article shows three example intentions a Qiari user might actually pick.
- If the topic is mechanism-of-action ("how does remote frequency wellness work"), be honest that the mechanism is contested in mainstream science and frame it as experiential / wellness rather than mechanistic / medical.
