# Pillar Article Type

Pillar pieces are cornerstone content. They define a topic completely so other articles can link back to them. They rank for high-intent informational queries and become hubs for topical authority.

## Structure expectations
- 2,000 to 2,500 words.
- 7-9 H2 sections, each substantive (not skimmed).
- Use H3 sub-sections inside the longer H2s where natural.
- Open with a concrete scene, observation, or pointed question that frames the topic. Not a definition.
- Define the key concept early but distinctively (not a Wikipedia paraphrase).
- Include at least one section that compares/contrasts with adjacent concepts so readers locate it on their mental map.
- Include at least one section addressing common skepticism honestly.
- Close with a CTA section that invites the reader into a Qiari session, using "RESONANCE" once.

## Pillar-specific guidance
- Establish Qiari (or the topic) as a category, not just a product. Pillar pieces should feel like the canonical reference.
- Link out generously to adjacent Qiari articles (those will rank too — pillar piece is the hub).
- This is the article that gets linked to from every related blog post for years. Treat it that way.
