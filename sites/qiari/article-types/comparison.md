# Comparison Article Type (X vs Y)

Comparison articles capture high-intent commercial queries. Reader is choosing between options.

## Structure expectations
- 1,400 to 2,000 words.
- 5-7 H2 sections.
- Open with a concrete user scenario where someone is genuinely deciding between the two options. Not "in the world of frequency wellness, two leading brands stand out".
- Define both options briefly and fairly in the first 200 words.
- Compare on dimensions that actually matter to the buyer (price, format, hardware required, personalization, learning curve, who it's for). Avoid puff dimensions.
- Use a comparison-table-as-prose paragraph or a clearly structured H3 per dimension. Do NOT use a literal markdown table as the article structure.
- Be honest about what the competitor does well. Pretending they have no merits damages credibility and Google notices.
- Land on a "who should pick which" recommendation, not a hard sell.
- Closing CTA section uses "RESONANCE" once.

## Comparison-specific guidance
- If comparing Qiari vs a competitor, NEVER make false claims about the competitor. Stick to publicly verifiable facts (pricing on their site, feature presence/absence, format).
- If comparing Qiari vs Qi Coil (sibling product), frame as different entry points, not competing products.
- The honest "who should pick what" framing is what makes these rank — Google's reviews update rewards genuine comparison over self-serving spin.
